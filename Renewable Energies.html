Advantages and Disadvantages
Things to Consider

Advantages of renewable energies:

  • It never runs out.
  • Very reliable; trade laws, political instabilities, and territorial claims cannot impact the use of renewable energy.
  • Environmentally friendly - minimal carbon and greenhouse gas emissions compared to fossil fuels.
  • Improves public health.
  • Creates lots of jobs.
  • Less maintenance cost compared to fossil fuel.
  • Reduces the ups and downs of energy prices; it does not depend on fuel prices.

Disadvantages of renewable energy:

  • Not available round the clock and depends on weather conditions.
  • Efficiency is low compared to fossil fuels.
  • The initial cost is high.
  • Requires a lot of space.
  • Renewable energy devices need recycling, e.g., solar cells.

Solar Energy
What About Solar Panels?

  • The Sun is always radiating heat and light during the day. We can utilize this sun’s energy in different ways.
  • Photovoltaic cells can convert sunlight into electricity. A group of photovoltaic cells are known as solar panels.
  • Homeowners can have solar panels installed on their houses and buy less electricity from their utility companies.

Wind Energy Discover Wind Turbines


A wind turbine is a device that converts the kinetic energy of wind into electrical energy.

Things to consider for wind turbines:

  • Lots of residential properties are not suitable for wind turbines.
  • You must have enough wind in your area.
  • Your house must be located on at least 1 acre of land.
  • Local zoning codes in your area allow wind turbines.
  • Must be comfortable with long-term investments.
  • Your average electricity bills are high, or you don't have access to utility grid power.


Hydropower
The Power of Water

All streams and rivers flow downhill. Before the water flows down the hill, it has potential energy because of its height.

Hydropower systems convert this potential energy into kinetic energy in a turbine, which drives a generator to produce electricity. The greater the height and the more water there is flowing through the turbine, the more electricity generated.





Geothermal
About Geothermal Heat Pumps

According to epa.gov, “Geothermal technology harnesses the earth’s heat. Just a few feet below the surface, the earth maintains a near-constant temperature, in contrast to the summer and winter extremes of the ambient air above ground.” This technology uses the natural earth’s heat to create heating, and cooling of the homes and the needed hot water for usage.





Biomass
What is it?

Biomass is a renewable energy source, generated from burning wood, plants, and other organic matter, such as manure or household waste. It releases carbon dioxide (CO2) when burned, but considerably less than fossil fuels.






Biogas
All You Need to Know

Biogas is a renewable fuel that's produced when organic matter, such as food or animal waste, is broken down by microorganisms in the absence of oxygen. This process is called anaerobic digestion. Additionally, this process also produces an organic, nutrient-rich liquid fertilizer.

Biogas is ideal for farms and restaurants that produce more organic waste and have a need for larger amounts of bio-fertilizer.
